A successful MVP is not a smaller version of your full product. It is the shortest path to validated learning.

## Start with outcomes

Define what must be true after launch:

- You can attract early users
- You can observe behavior with analytics
- You can deliver one complete user journey

## Prioritize feature tiers

Use three buckets for planning:

- **Must have:** Core functionality required for launch
- **Should have:** High-value improvements for iteration two
- **Could have:** Nice-to-haves that do not block validation

## Keep architecture practical

Choose tools your team can maintain. Reliability and speed of iteration are more important than novelty in early stages.

## Final thought

The MVP should answer one question clearly: is this product solving a real user problem?
